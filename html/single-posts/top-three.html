My top 3 football players

By Berty Pérez

I've been following football for a long time, when I was a child and started to play football I didn't have a woman player as a role model to follow, back in the days we didn't have the media covering professional women's players, so it was hard to keep track of them. I'm extremely happy that nowadays is more common to watch national and international leagues thanks to the internet and the streaming platforms. So, here are my top 3 football players around the world (no specific order).

Lizbeth Ovalle

Ovalle is a Mexican football player, she plays as a midfielder for Tigres UANL femenil and she is also a player for the México National Team. She is from Aguascalientes and she has 23 years old. To me Ovalle is the best midfielder in México, we call her "la maga" which means "the magician", because that is was she does, she does magic with her football and her feet, watching her play is always a great show. I hope one day I can meet her.

She has a long journey ahead of her and I hope I can see her in the future playing in the NSFW or in Europe, playing the Women's Champions

She has won five championships with Tigres UANL femenil

Marta Vieira da Silva

Marta is a brazilian professional football player who has inspired tons of girls across the world, her story is very touching and the way she manage to persuit her dream of become a professional player is admirable. When Marta was a child, she was told many times that football was only for boys, and she must quit playing, but that didn't stop her, when she was 20 years old she won FIFA's Best Player. She plays as a striker.

To me Marta is one of the most iconic female player, because she stands up for women's football rights, she has also scored on every Women's World Cup. On the last Women's World Cup she said something that I really felt inside my soul.

“It's training more. It's taking care of yourself more. It's being ready to play ninety plus thirty minutes. This is what I ask of the girls. There's not going to be a Formiga forever. There's not going to be a Marta forever. There's not going to be a Cristiane. The women's game depends on you to survive.”

Even now I read this and my skin crawls, because this is what I would wanted to hear when I was I child, and this is a great message for the new generation to hear.

Marta plays for the Orlando Pride team in USA and she is also selected with the Brazilian National Team

Megan Rapinoe

Megan is an American (from USA) football player, she is currently part of the OL Reign team, her position is midfielder and she is a really good one, one of the bests globally. She was a key player on the last Women's World Cup (2019), she is also an activist for the LGTBI community, she stands by her ideals, she speaks up against unfairness. She also plays for the USA National Team, one of the best teams of the world. She seems to be a humble person with her feet on the ground.

Rapinoe was part of the women's team's equal pay complaint to the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission since 2016, where the female players were asking for equal pay as their male companions, this is something that not many people understans, but the National female team in USA have won more cups than the National male team, so it doesn't makes sense that the National female team earns less than their male companions
